About

IoT CLUB

            TCE IoT Club is a community of students passionate about the idea of everyday physical objects being connected and communicating through internet. The internet of Things is everywhere and it's tireless expansion is fascinating to witness. This club is an active platform for students to display and develop their practical IoT device building skills and knowledge

            The IoT Club of TCE strives to stimulate interest in Internet of Things and related domains among all the students. Besides making a serious venture to spread knowledge on IoT and it's diverse applications, we also stand by organized and disciplined work structure which is this commendable virtue that has bonded the club together and helped it to emerge to it's full potential

Hardwares

Arduino UNO R3

An open-source microcontroller board based on the Microchip ATmega328P microcontroller and developed by Arduino.cc.

Arduino Due

It is a microcontroller board based on the Atmel SAM3X8E ARM Cortex-M3 CPU. ... The Arduino Due is programmed using the Arduino Software (IDE)that is common to all our boards

Arduino Nano

It comes with a crystal oscillator of frequency 16 MHz. It is used to produce a clock of precise frequency using constant voltage. The programming of Nano can be done with a mini USB cable

Capacitors in a circuit board

a passive two-terminal electrical component used to store energy electrostatically in an electric field. A a capacitor stores energy in the form of an electrostatic field between its plates.

Rasberry Pi 3

A credit-card sized computer that plugs into a computer monitor or TV, and uses a standard keyboard and mouse. It enables people to explore computing, and to learn how to program.

Microcontroller

a compact integrated circuit designed to govern a specific operation in an embedded system. A typical microcontroller includes a processor, memory and input/output (I/O) peripherals on a single chip.
